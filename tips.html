Money-Saving Tips

Smart strategies to make your pocket money go further

Filter by:
Featured Tip

The 50/30/20 Rule for Pocket Money

Divide your pocket money into three categories: 50% for needs (lunch, transportation), 30% for wants (entertainment, treats), and 20% for savings. This simple rule helps you balance spending while building good saving habits.

Planning

Pack Your Lunch

Bringing lunch from home instead of buying it at school or outside can save you $20-$50 per week. That's up to $200 a month!

Daily Habits

Wait 24 Hours Before Buying

Before making a non-essential purchase, wait 24 hours. This "cooling off" period helps avoid impulse buying and gives you time to decide if you really need it.

Shopping

Plan Your Expenses Weekly

Take 10 minutes every Sunday to plan your expenses for the week. Knowing what's coming up helps you avoid overspending and prepare for necessary costs.

Planning

Sell Items You No Longer Need

Books, clothes, toys, or electronics you no longer use can be sold online or at yard sales. This declutters your space while earning extra money.

Earning

Use a Reusable Water Bottle

Buying bottled water can cost $1-$3 per day. A reusable water bottle pays for itself within a week and helps the environment too.

Daily Habits

Look for Student Discounts

Many stores, restaurants, and services offer student discounts. Always ask or check online before purchasing - your student ID could save you 10-20%.

Shopping

Save First, Spend Later

As soon as you receive your pocket money, set aside your savings portion first. This "pay yourself first" approach ensures you build savings before spending.

Planning

Offer Services in Your Neighborhood

Lawn mowing, pet sitting, babysitting, or helping with technology can earn you extra money while building valuable skills and references.

Earning

Monthly Saving Challenge

This month's challenge: No-Spend Weekends

Choose one weekend each week where you spend $0. Find free activities, use what you have at home, and discover how creative you can be without spending!
